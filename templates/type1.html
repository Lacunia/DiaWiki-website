Type I Diabetes

This is also known as “juvenile” diabetes. It is a type of autoimmune disease, meaning that the body self attacks, where the cells that produce insulin in the pancreas are attacked and destroyed by your own body. Type 1 diabetes is commonly diagnosed in children and young adults and is more of a genetic condition.

Symptoms

Symptoms for type 1 diabetes develop quickly, usually over a few weeks or months.

Causes

Here are some causes of type 1 diabetes:
  • Family history of type 1 diabetes
  • Injured pancreas
  • Presence of autoantibodies (something that attacks your own body's tissues or organs)
  • Physical stress
  • Exposure to illnesses caused by viruses
