Gestational Diabetes

This type of diabetes only develops in some women during the pregnancy period. The reason for it is because the hormones produced by your body during pregnancy make the body's cells more resistant to insulin. Gestational diabetes usually goes away after pregnancy, but it will increase your risk of developing type 2 diabetes later in life.

Symptoms

Symptoms for gestational diabetes are usually unnoticeable. Your obstetrician is responsible for testing you for this type of diabetes during your pregnancy.

Causes

Here are some causes of gestational diabetes:
  • Family history of gestational diabetes
  • Overweight (before the pregnancy)
