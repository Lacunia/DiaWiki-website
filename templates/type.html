Types of Diabetes

Diabetes is a disease caused by the buildup of extra sugar (glucose) in the bloodstream, which can not be absorbed by the body and be used for energy. When glucose is in the bloodstream, insulin, realized by the pancreas, is responsible for carrying the glucose to its destination. However, when your pancreas doesn’t make enough insulin, or when your body cells don’t respond to and use the insulin, then glucose would remain in your bloodstream.

Common Symptoms

  • Increased thirst
  • Weak and tired feelings
  • Blurred vision
  • Numbness or tingling in the hands or feet
  • Slow-healing sores or cuts
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Frequent urination
  • Frequent unexplained infections
  • Dry mouth

Explore the sections below to learn more about each type specifically.

Type I Diabetes

Type 1 diabetes is a genetic condition that shows up in early life.

Type II Diabetes

Type 2 diabetes is developed over time during one's life time.

Gestational Diabetes

Gestational diabetes is a type of diabetes that only develop in pregnant women.
